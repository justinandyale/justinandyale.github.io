Why Yale? Why Justin?

  • Biology and the World - I'm one of (what seems like) only a handful of high schoolers interested in studying the growing issue of antibiotic-resistance, which, if left unchecked, may cause more deaths than cancer by 2050. As a student at Yale, I could take advantage of the unique biology track, biotechnology, and prepare myself for my career in researching alternatives to antibiotics.
  • Liberal Arts Core - My brain speaks biology, but my soul speaks art. At Yale, I plan to puruse my diverse interests in creative writing, art history, and politics as I become a more "broadly informed, and highly disciplined intellect." Perhaps by the end of my four years, Yale will boast yet another young author: I hope to publish a book I've started planning - "The Torturer's Apprentice" - by the time I complete my degree.
  • Community Engagement - I am a man who lives to serve. At Yale, this means continuing my devotion to teaching high schoolers about computer science by helping to lead clubs such as Code Haven and Code for Good. I would also like to be involved in the Bioethics Society and help solve the growing concerns that follow newer biotechnologies.
